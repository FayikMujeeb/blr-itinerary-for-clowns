Mysore 🏰

The one big out-of-city day. The City of Palaces — royal, dramatic, and 100% worth the early alarm. Here's the full plan.

~140 kmfrom Bangalore
~3 hrseach way
6:30 AMideal departure
1 daytotally doable
6:30 AM

Depart Whitefield

Early start beats the traffic and the heat. A pre-booked cab is easiest for a group of 3; train is the budget option from KSR Bengaluru.

10:00 AM

Mysore Palace

The main event — Amba Vilas Palace, a masterpiece of Indo-Saracenic architecture with stained glass, carved ceilings & chandeliers. Go in the morning before it's packed. Bring your camera; this is THE photo spot.

12:30 PM

Lunch + Devaraja Market

Authentic South Indian lunch in the city centre, then wander the century-old market for silk sarees, sandalwood and color-everywhere flower stalls.

2:00 PM

Chamundi Hills

13 km up for the Chamundeshwari Temple, panoramic views over Mysore, and the giant Nandi statue halfway down. Drive up (skip the 1,000 steps unless you're feeling it).

4:30 PM

St. Philomena's Church

One of India's largest cathedrals — neo-gothic twin spires inspired by Cologne Cathedral. Quick but stunning photo stop.

5:30 PM

Brindavan Gardens (optional)

If energy allows: landscaped gardens by the KRS dam, famous for the evening musical fountain show. Adds ~1 hr but it's magical. Otherwise, start the drive home.

Evening

Drive back to Bangalore

Nap in the car. You earned it. 💤

⚡ The honest take

It's a long day — early out, late back. If you'd rather go gentler, you can drop Brindavan Gardens and St. Philomena's and just do Palace + Chamundi Hills + market at a relaxed pace. Either way, the palace alone makes the trip.
